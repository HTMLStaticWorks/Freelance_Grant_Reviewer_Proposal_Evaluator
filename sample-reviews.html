Key Strengths Identified

✓ Strength – Need Statement (Score: 88)

"The problem statement is compelling and data-rich. You effectively document unemployment rates (23.4%) in the target zip codes, citing Census data and local workforce board reports. The connection between structural barriers and program design is explicit and persuasive."

✓ Strength – Project Design (Score: 91)

"The 12-week training model is well-sequenced and grounded in evidence-based practices. The employer partnership letters are exceptional—three Fortune 500 companies committing to interview program graduates significantly strengthens your application."

✓ Strength – Budget Narrative (Score: 86)

"Budget line items are clearly justified and cost-per-participant ($4,200) is competitive with comparable programs. The in-kind match from corporate partners adds significant credibility and demonstrates funder confidence."

Areas Needing Improvement

⚠ Weakness – Evaluation Plan (Score: 75)

"The evaluation plan mentions tracking outcomes but lacks SMART goals. You state you will 'track employment rates' but do not specify a target percentage, baseline, or data collection instrument. Add at least 5 measurable outputs with baseline data."

⚠ Weakness – Sustainability Plan (Score: 79)

"The sustainability section is vague—'we plan to seek continued funding' is insufficient. Identify at least 2 specific alternative funders, describe your earned income potential, and provide a 3-year financial projection that shows reduced grant dependency."

Sample Inline Annotations (Page 4, Section 2)

ORIGINAL PROPOSAL TEXT – Section 2.1: Target Population
"Our organization serves young adults in the community who face challenges in finding employment. We will work with approximately 75 participants over the course of the grant period to help them find jobs in various industries. The program will prepare them for careers that pay living wages. Our track record shows we have successfully served this population before and are well-positioned to deliver results."
NOTE 1 'Young adults' needs age definition per WIOA Section 129 eligibility criteria (14–24). State explicitly: "youth ages 18–24."
NOTE 2 Name your 3 target sectors (e.g., healthcare support, tech, skilled trades). Justify selection with local labor market data showing job growth.

After Revision
Section 3.2 – Evaluation Plan (Revised)
"We will measure program effectiveness through five SMART outcomes: (1) 80% of participants will complete all 12 training modules (baseline: 65%); (2) 75% will earn an industry-recognized credential within 16 weeks (baseline: 0%); (3) 70% will secure employment within 90 days (baseline: 42%). Data will be collected via pre/post assessments, Salesforce CRM tracking, and 6-month follow-up surveys conducted by an independent evaluator."
